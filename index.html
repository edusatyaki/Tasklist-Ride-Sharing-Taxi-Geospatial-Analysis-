Section 01
Problem Statement
Core Problem
New York City's yellow taxi industry operates one of the world's largest urban transportation networks. In 2025, 48.7 million trips generated over $1 billion in estimated revenue — yet demand is highly non-uniform across hours, days, boroughs, and seasons. The newly introduced CBD Congestion Fee ($0.75/trip) effective January 2025 adds a new revenue and behavioral variable never seen in prior years' data. This project answers: Where, when, and why do trip volumes and fares surge — and how can the TLC, drivers, and fleet operators use data to make better decisions?
Stakeholders
Primary: TLC Policy Analysts, Fleet Operations Managers

Secondary: Individual drivers optimizing shift timing, Rideshare investors, City transport planners, Fare pricing teams
Dataset Context
Source: NYC TLC Trip Record Data (official)
Format: 12 Parquet files (Jan–Dec 2025)
Size: 48.7M rows × 20 columns
New in 2025: cbd_congestion_fee column
Research Questions
Six Key Business Questions
Q1
Demand Patterns
Which hours, days, and months have highest trip demand? What drives the May–Jun summer peak and Dec holiday surge?
Q2
Revenue Drivers
What combination of distance, duration, payment type, and surcharges drives the highest total fares?
Q3
Surge & Pricing
Do late-night and early-morning trips command higher tip rates? Is there a price sensitivity pattern?
Q4
CBD Fee Impact
How has the new $0.75 Jan 2025 CBD congestion fee changed trip behaviour, volumes, and revenue in Manhattan?
Q5
Geographic Hotspots
Which pickup/dropoff zones generate the most revenue? How do JFK, LaGuardia, and Midtown compare?
Q6
Payment Behavior
Does credit card vs cash split affect average fare? Which zones and hours have highest tipping rates?

Section 02
Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering
Key Data Quality Issues Found in This Dataset
Negative fares: 144K+ rows with fare_amount < 0 — likely disputed/refund transactions. Drop.
Extreme outliers: fare max = $863,372 and distance max = 276,423 miles — clearly corrupt. Cap at $500 / 100 miles.
Zero-distance trips: 90K+ trips with distance = 0 but non-zero fare — keep only if fare < $5 (meter starts), else investigate.
Negative durations: 124 rows where dropoff < pickup — timezone errors. Drop.
passenger_count nulls: 540K nulls per month (from VendorID=2 not reporting). Fill with median.

# ── Load & combine all 12 months ────────────────────────── import pandas as pd, numpy as np, glob files = sorted(glob.glob('data/raw/yellow_tripdata_2025-*.parquet')) dfs = [] for i, f in enumerate(files): tmp = pd.read_parquet(f) tmp['month'] = i + 1 dfs.append(tmp) df = pd.concat(dfs, ignore_index=True) # ── Feature engineering example ─────────────────────────── df['fare_per_mile'] = df['fare_amount'] / df['trip_distance'] df['tip_pct'] = np.where(df['payment_type']==1, df['tip_amount']/df['fare_amount']*100, np.nan) df['is_cbd'] = (df['cbd_congestion_fee'] > 0).astype(int) df.to_parquet('data/cleaned/nyc_taxi_2025_clean.parquet')
